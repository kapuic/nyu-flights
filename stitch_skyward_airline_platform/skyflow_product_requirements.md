# Product Requirements Document: SkyFlow Airline Product

## 1. Overview
SkyFlow is a modern airline reservation platform divided into two primary experiences: a unified Traveler Product and a Staff Operations Dashboard. The goal is to move away from "course-project" aesthetics toward a professional, product-grade interface that feels authoritative and efficient.

## 2. Traveler Experience (Consumer-Facing)
### 2.1 Unified Journey
*   **Search & Discovery:** Open to anonymous visitors. High-focus, conversion-oriented search (Source, Destination, Dates, Trip Type).
*   **Progressive Authentication:** Login is only required for gated actions (booking, viewing history, managing account).
*   **Search Results:** Scannable cards/rows communicating flight number, airline, times, price, status, seat availability, and ratings.
*   **Booking Flow:** A natural continuation of search, not a context switch.

### 2.2 My Trips & History
*   **Upcoming vs. Past:** Strong visual separation.
*   **Review System:** Reviews (1-5 stars + comment) are only enabled for completed, unreviewed trips.

## 3. Staff Experience (Internal Ops)
### 3.1 Two-Panel Layout
*   **Main Workspace:** Operational tasks (scheduled flights, toggling status, passenger lists, fleet management).
*   **Context Rail:** Persistent KPIs, airline context, and quick actions.
### 3.2 Key Functionalities
*   **Flight Management:** 30-day view, create future flights, toggle delay/on-time status.
*   **Fleet Management:** Add airplanes (seat count, manufacturing info).
*   **Reporting:** Sales metrics (total, monthly, yearly) with trend charts and date-range filters.
*   **Feedback Monitoring:** Review customer ratings and comments.

## 4. Visual Identity
*   **Traveler:** Polished, calm, search-first.
*   **Staff:** Structured, efficient, dashboard-first.
*   **Constraints:** No generic "startup" fluff; focus on typography, spacing, and information architecture.
